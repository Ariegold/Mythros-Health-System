Terms of Use & Medical Disclaimer
Please read carefully before using this service
⚠ Medical Disclaimer
  • Mythros Health System is not a medical device and does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
  • All interpretations are for informational and educational purposes only to help you understand your results.
  • Always consult a qualified healthcare professional before making any decisions about your health.
  • In an emergency, call your local emergency services immediately. Do not rely on this app.
  • This service is not a substitute for professional medical care, advice, or clinical judgement.
What this service does
  • Explains lab test results in plain, simple language you can understand.
  • Flags values that are normal, borderline (Watch), or significantly abnormal (Urgent).
  • Uses country-specific reference ranges (Nigeria, UK, USA and more).
  • Supports 15+ languages to help non-English speakers access their results.
  • Interprets genomic and pharmacogenomics results including sickle cell genotype, MODY, BRCA, Factor V Leiden, CYP2D6, and more — with population-diverse African ancestry context informed by HGSVC3 reference data.
Your data & privacy
  • Your lab results are processed to generate your interpretation and are not stored long-term.
  • Anonymous usage data (no personal details) is collected for service improvement.
  • We do not sell your data to any third party.
  • This service is operated by Mythros Support Services Ltd (Company No. 16384279, UK). Full privacy policy available at mythrosltd.co.uk.
🧬 Genomic & Pharmacogenomics Results
  • Genomic interpretations (e.g. BRCA, Lynch Syndrome, sickle cell, MODY) are for informational purposes and do not constitute a genetic counselling service.
  • Results classified as Pathogenic or Likely Pathogenic should be discussed with a clinical genetics specialist or your GP.
  • A Variant of Uncertain Significance (VUS) is not a diagnosis — it means more evidence is needed and should not be acted on alone.
  • Pharmacogenomics results (e.g. CYP2D6, CYP2C19, TPMT) inform medication safety — always tell your prescriber about any pharmacogenomics findings before starting a new medication.
  • Inherited cancer results (BRCA, Lynch Syndrome) have implications for blood relatives — discuss with a specialist about family cascade testing.
